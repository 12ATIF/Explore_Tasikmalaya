About Tasikmalaya

Where Nature Meets
Ancient Traditions

Nestled in the heart of West Java, Kabupaten Tasikmalaya is a land of extraordinary natural beauty and profound cultural heritage. Home to the majestic Gunung Galunggung volcano, the pristine traditional village of Kampung Naga, and the stunning Pantai Cipatujah coastline, Tasikmalaya offers a journey through Indonesia's soul.

As one of Indonesia's emerging smart tourism destinations, Tasikmalaya blends centuries of Sundanese wisdom with modern digital innovation — preserving traditions while embracing the future. It is home to world-renowned bamboo craftsmanship, exquisite batik artistry, and a culinary heritage that tells the story of a resilient and creative people.

Culture & Heritage

Stories of Sundanese Soul

Centuries of wisdom, artistry, and tradition woven into the fabric of daily life

Tradition

Bamboo Craft Artistry

For generations, Tasikmalaya artisans have transformed humble bamboo into exquisite works of art. From intricate woven baskets to elegant furniture, this living tradition showcases extraordinary Sundanese craftsmanship recognized worldwide.

Heritage

Batik Tasikmalaya

Each stroke of the canting tool tells a story. Tasikmalaya's batik tradition carries distinct patterns inspired by nature and spirituality, reflecting the deep connection between the Sundanese people and their land.

Culture

Sundanese Way of Life

In villages like Kampung Naga, the Sundanese philosophy of living in harmony with nature remains vibrant. Communities maintain ancestral customs, traditional architecture, and spiritual practices passed down through centuries.

Culinary Discovery

Taste of Tasikmalaya

Authentic Sundanese flavors that tell the story of this land

Nasi Tutug Oncom

Steamed rice mixed with fermented soybean (oncom) — a fragrant, umami-rich staple that is the heart and soul of Tasikmalaya cuisine. Often served with fried chicken and sambal.

🌶 Signature Must Try

Kupat Tahu

Rice cakes served with fried tofu, bean sprouts, and a luscious peanut sauce. This beloved street food is a perfect blend of textures and flavors, found in every corner of the city.

🥜 Street Food Popular

Opak

Thin, crispy traditional crackers made from glutinous rice, sun-dried and roasted to perfection. A beloved snack that showcases the simplicity and elegance of Sundanese culinary artistry.
